    True beauty devotees know their way around best Korean skincare brands and have top shelves full of the best Korean face and sheet masks, Korean cleansing oils, and Korean sunscreens. But if they narrowed their K-Beauty collections down to a single product, the best Korean moisturizer would remain front and center. Moisturizers are a necessity in this beauty realm: “The Korean philosophy for skincare is very hydration focused,” explains Alicia Yoon, founder of K-beauty brand Peach & Lily.

    All the best Korean moisturizers are designed to repair the skin barrier, soothe skin, and provide a plumped-up appearance. Some are formulated to reinvigorate dry, flaky skin, while others are a holy grail for hydrating oily skin without any excess shine. From cult-favorite K-beauty brands like LaNeige and Sulwhasoo to up-and-coming lines like Hanacure, Marie Claire editors consulted the K-beauty experts and vetted shopper reviews to find the best Korean moisturizers for quenching your skin stateside.

    The Best Korean Moisturizers

    The Best Korean Moisturizer With Green Tea

    Innisfree Intensive Hydrating Cream with Green Tea Seed

    With combination skin, Beauty Writer Gabrielle Ulubay has struggled to find a moisturizer that suits all her needs at once, particularly as skin changes in along with the weather, her routine, or her stress level. This moisturizer is an outlier. "Innisfree's green tea moisturizer is one of those rare gems that's able to moisturize my skin without breaking me out, no matter what external factors are affecting my complexion," she says. "Suitable for all skin types, it stays true to its promise of evening out skin tone. Plus, it makes my skin unbelievably soft to the touch."

    Pros: Deeply hydrating; Builds back moisture barrier 

    Cons: Some report irritation

    Customer Review: "It’s creamy and light weight but makes my skin feel incredibly hydrated. I love using it under my makeup with a dewy highlighter base because it gives my face the natural 'lit from within' look. Loved using it in France in the cold. My face gets really dry when temperatures drop and this made my skin feel hydrated all day!" — Sephora

The matcha helps with any inflammation and doesn't add to the distress on my skin. Yoon says the ingredient is also is "a rich antioxidant that helps combat external stressors as well as visibly calms skin."

    What to Look For

    • Hydrating Ingredients 

    “Korean beauty moisturizers strive to ensure deep hydration regardless of texture or if the formula is oil-based or oil-free,” says Yoon. So, whether you need a lightweight Korean moisturizer for oily skin or have dry skin and require a rich, peptide-packed formula, your favorite Korean moisturizer will include hydrating ingredients.

    This is largely up to personal preference, but texture is huge factor when selecting the best Korean moisturizer for you. Marie Claire's edit includes some gel and water creams, ideal for summer months or for those with oilier skin types, as well as rich creams and emollients, which are suited to dry skin. If you have combination skin, assess your current needs (if you're going to the gym a lot, for instance, you might want a more lightweight option), or layer a lightweight moisture with a more moisture-packed face serum.
