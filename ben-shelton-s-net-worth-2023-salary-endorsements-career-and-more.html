    Ben Shelton is an American tennis player who was born in Atlanta, America. He started playing regularly at the age of 12, and achieved a career-high singles ranking of world No. 35 on May 8, 2023. In doubles, he achieved a career-high ranking of world No. 90 in August 2023. Shelton won the USTA Junior National Doubles Championship in 2016 and represented his college team Florida Gators in many tennis tournaments. He won the 2022 NCAA Division 1 championship in singles.

    Shelton made his ATP debut at the 2022 Atlanta Open, where he defeated Ramkumar Ramanathan and announced the news of turning professional in August 2022. He is represented by Alessandro Sant Albano, who was part of Roger Federer's management team.

    Shelton rose to prominence when he defeated World No. 5 Casper Ruud in the third round of the Cincinnati Masters. His best Grand Slam performance came at the 2023 Australian Open, where he lost to Tommy Paul in four sets.
