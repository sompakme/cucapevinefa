    How to Fix / Solve: Fortnite Matchmaking Error #4

    Are you an avid Fortnite player facing the frustrating matchmaking error #4? Dont worry, youre not alone! This article will guide you through simple steps to tackle the issue and get you back into the gaming action in no time.

    21 Sep 2024, 00:00

    Are you an avid Fortnite player facing the frustrating matchmaking error #4? Don’t worry, you’re not alone! This article will guide you through simple steps to tackle the issue and get you back into the gaming action in no time.

    Change Your Matchmaking Region

    If you’re stuck with error #4, try switching your matchmaking region. Here’s how:
    Open the game menu and find the “Settings” option.
    Look for the “gear icon” to access your settings.
    Select a different region from the available list.

    Verify Game Files

    Corrupted game files can trigger errors. Follow these steps:
    On PC, open the Epic Games Launcher.
    Navigate to your library and find Fortnite.
    Click on the three dots below the game and select “Verify.”

    Disable Your VPN

    Using a VPN might be causing the issue. Disable it by:
    Turning off your VPN service.
    Restart Fortnite and try matchmaking again.

    Check Fortnite Server Status

    Before making changes, ensure the problem isn’t with Fortnite servers:
    Visit the Epic Games Public Status page.
    Follow Fortnite Status on Twitter for updates.
    Check community forums for user discussions.
    Use third-party sites like Downdetector Fortnite page.

    Check Your Internet Connection

    A stable internet connection is crucial:
    Restart your router by unplugging and waiting for a minute.
    Reconnect and try joining Fortnite.
    Perform a speed test to ensure a stable connection.

    Restart Fortnite and Your Device

    A quick restart can fix temporary glitches:
    Close Fortnite completely.
    Restart your gaming device.
    Launch Fortnite and attempt to enter a match.

    Update Fortnite

    Keeping the game updated is vital for bug fixes:
    On PC:
    Open the Epic Games Launcher.
    Locate Fortnite in the “Library” section.
    Click “Update” if available.
    On Consoles:
    Go to your game library.
    Highlight Fortnite and select “Check for Update.”
    On Mobile Devices:
    Open the App Store or Google Play Store.
    Search for Fortnite and tap “Update” if available.

    Change DNS Servers

    Enhance network stability by switching DNS servers:
    Access your device’s network settings.
    Navigate to the properties of your active network connection.
    Manually specify DNS servers (e.g., 8.8.8.8 for Google’s DNS).
    Save changes and restart your network connection if needed.

    Uninstall and Reinstall Fortnite

    If all else fails, reinstalling Fortnite might help:
    On PC:
    Open the Epic Games Launcher.
    Find Fortnite and click on the three dots.
    Choose “Uninstall” and then reinstall.
    On Consoles:
    Delete or uninstall Fortnite.
    Go to the console’s store and redownload the game.
    On Mobile Devices (Android):
    Open the Google Play Store.
    Search for Fortnite and tap “Uninstall.”
    Install it again.

    Need Further Help?

    If none of the above works, reach out to Epic Games Support:
    Visit the Epic Games Support Center.
    Submit a request for assistance with your matchmaking error.

    Conclusion:

    Dealing with Fortnite’s matchmaking error #4 can be frustrating, but these simple steps should help you overcome the issue. Switch regions, verify files, ensure a stable internet, and keep your game updated. If you’re still stuck, don’t hesitate to contact Epic Games Support for personalized help. Happy gaming!

    FAQs

    Q: How long does it take to fix Fortnite’s matchmaking error #4?
    A: The duration varies based on the error’s cause and troubleshooting. Follow the steps in this article, and if needed, contact Epic Games Support for additional assistance.
    Q: Can changing DNS servers resolve Fortnite’s error #4?
    A: Yes, using a public DNS server may improve network stability and resolve various matchmaking errors, including error #4.
    Q: Will reinstalling Fortnite erase my progress?
    A: No, your game data is linked to your Epic Games account. Reinstalling won’t erase progress; just log in with the same account after reinstalling.
